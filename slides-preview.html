What is a Machine?
AI History & Foundations
We begin where all technology begins — understanding what machines are, what computers do, and how the question "Can machines think?" changed everything.
Module 1 › What is a Machine?
Defining the Machine
"

A machine is anything that reduces human effort.

— Rancho, 3 Idiots (2009)
"

A machine is a device, having a unique purpose, that augments or replaces human or animal effort for the accomplishment of physical tasks.

— Britannica
💡 From the plow to the smartphone — machines have always extended what humans can do. Every new tool answers the same question: How can we do more with less effort?
What is a Computer?
An electronic device that can receive, store, process, and present data under the control of a program.
Step 1

Raw data, concepts, ideas, materials
Step 2

Thinking, computing, transforming

?

Artificial
Something made, produced, or done by humans — especially to seem like something natural.
Intelligence
The human ability to perform cognitive tasks — to think, understand, learn, and remember.
Combine both concepts → Artificial Intelligence
The Father of Modern Computing
Alan
Turing
1912 – 1954
[Photo]
His Contributions
🔐
WWII Code-Breaking
Decrypted the Nazi Enigma machine, enabling the Allies to read encrypted military communications and helping bring the war to an earlier end.
💻
Computing Theory
Laid the mathematical foundation for modern computers with the concept of the "Turing Machine" — a theoretical model of computation.
🧠
AI Pioneer
Asked "Can machines think?" in 1950 and proposed a practical test to evaluate machine intelligence — now known as the Turing Test.
The Turing Test
Can a machine's responses be indistinguishable from a human's?

Limitations of the Test

The "confederate effect" — humans sometimes misidentified as machines

The Story of AI
From a bold question in 1950 to everyday reality
🌱
1940s – 50s
Birth of AI
Turing Test proposed, Dartmouth Conference held, "Artificial Intelligence" coined by John McCarthy (1956).
❄️
1960s – 90s
Early Programs & AI Winters
ELIZA (1966), Deep Blue defeats Kasparov (1997), but two "AI winters" of funding cuts slowed progress.
⚡
2000s – 10s
Modern Breakthrough
IBM Watson wins Jeopardy! Siri & Alexa arrive. Deep learning transforms image recognition. AlphaGo (2016).
🚀
2020 – Now
Generative AI Era
ChatGPT, DALL-E, Gemini. LLMs like GPT-4 with 175B+ parameters generate human-like text, images & code.
