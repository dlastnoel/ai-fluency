## The 8 Applications of AI in Education

### 1. Learning Process Personalization

**How it works:**
- AI platforms collect and analyze student data on:
  - Interaction with educational materials
  - Exercise completion time
  - Test results
  - Overall performance
- Generative AI tools design personalized training pathways
- Systems adapt in real-time to learner progress

**Example Application:**
When an AI-powered system detects a student learning English is having difficulties, it can:
- Generate personalized listening, speaking, reading, or writing exercises
- Provide immediate feedback upon completion
- Present content in various interactive formats to accommodate different learning styles

**Key Benefit:** Students receive content tailored to their individual needs and learning preferences.

---

### 2. Learning Outcomes Prediction

**How it works:**
- ML-based solutions process previously collected data on:
  - Students' academic performance
  - Attitudes
  - Social conditions
- Algorithms categorize students into different archetypes
- Systems compare and identify relationships between learner categories and their typical outcomes

**Applications:**
| Stakeholder | Use Case |
|-------------|----------|
| Professors | Choose best teaching methods and programs based on student skills and needs |
| Institutions | Identify at-risk students before it's too late—those likely to drop out, not graduate on time, or struggle with poor grades, absenteeism, or tardiness |

**Key Benefit:** Enables proactive intervention rather than reactive responses to student struggles.

---

### 3. Automation of Mundane Teacher Tasks

**Current capabilities:**
- Test evaluation with proper grading software
- Correcting multiple-choice and true-false exercises
- Preliminary assessment of short-written answers (improving with LLMs)
- Essay evaluation and writing skills assessment (evolving capability)

**Potential Impact (McKinsey & Company data):**
The research indicates significant potential for reallocation of working hours via automation technologies among K-12 teachers in Canada, Singapore, United Kingdom, and United States.

**Key Benefit:** Saves teacher time for more meaningful student interactions.

---

### 4. Administrative Workflows

**Automatable activities include:**
- Processing student applications
- Enrollment management
- Facility management
- HR procedures
- Recruiting
- Digital correspondence processing

**Real-World Example:**
The Department for Education of the United Kingdom partnered with Capgemini to adopt a system that:
- Autonomously processes digital correspondence
- Speeds up follow-up rate to incoming emails
- Combines robotic process automation (RPA) with AI enhancement

**Key Benefit:** Cuts costs, enhances management efficiency, improves responsiveness to student needs.

---

### 5. Assistance for Special Needs Students

**How AI helps:**
- Provides tailored learning paths and exercises
- Ensures equal opportunity access to education
- Accommodates various accessibility requirements

**Example Application:**
AI-enabled text-to-speech software can read instructional content aloud, providing students with visual impairments an equal opportunity to learn.

**Key Benefit:** Democratizes education by addressing individual accessibility needs.

---

### 6. Resource Planning

**AI capabilities:**
- Calculating the right amount of food, learning materials, or stationery
- Ensuring timely purchase and delivery
- Quickly adapting to new conditions (especially important post-pandemic)

**Tasks addressed:**
- Time-consuming calculations
- Error-prone manual planning
- Supply chain management for educational institutions

**Key Benefit:** Reduces human error and improves operational efficiency in school management.

---

### 7. Curriculum Design

**Why this is challenging:**
- Knowledge base constantly changes
- Relevance shifts over time
- Creating effective curricula requires processing vast amounts of data

**How AI helps:**
- Processes enormous amounts of data about:
  - Students' progress
  - Interests
  - Competencies
  - Challenges in a given year
- Identifies patterns and trends
- Assesses effectiveness of pedagogical approaches
- Predicts outcomes of different educational strategies

**Scale of impact:**
Can provide value from individual lesson plans to school-level or even national-level curriculum improvements.

**Key Benefit:** Data-driven curriculum decisions based on actual student outcomes rather than assumptions.

---

### 8. Ongoing Assistance During Learning

**Technology combination:**
- ML-driven adaptive learning
- Natural language processing
- Virtual assistants and chatbots

**Advantages:**
| Feature | Benefit |
|---------|---------|
| 24/7 availability | Support whenever questions arise |
| Non-judgmental | Students can experiment freely |
| Reduces anxiety | Less fear of making mistakes compared to classroom settings |
| Consistent feedback | Helps students keep up with studies |

**Key Benefit:** Always-available support that encourages experimentation and reduces learning anxiety.

## Emerging Technologies to Pair with AI

### Virtual Reality (VR)

**Definition:** Provides computer-generated 3D simulation of reality.

**Educational Value:**
- Enhances learning in medical and technical fields
- Provides immersive, interactive experiences simulating real-world scenarios
- Students can learn anatomy by exploring and interacting with 3D models of organs, bones, and body parts

### Augmented Reality (AR)

**Definition:** Overlays digital information onto a physical environment.

**Educational Value:**
- Superimposes digital information onto physical objects
- Allows students to learn more about subjects while experimenting in the real world

### Internet of Things (IoT)

**Definition:** Network of physical devices with sensors, software, and connectivity that collect and exchange data.

**Educational Value:**
- Provides real-time data from classrooms:
  - Lighting and temperature levels
  - Desk use
  - Student attendance
- Helps teachers and administrators understand how students learn in the classroom

## Real-Life Examples

### Duolingo

**Type:** Language-learning application with AI-powered personalization

**Key Features:**
- Famous tutor/mascot: A cheerful but insistent green owl that motivates daily exercises
- Initial positioning test to scan user's skills
- Progressive readjustment of activity difficulty based on:
  - Previous performance
  - Results achieved
- Points-based reward system to maintain engagement

**AI Approach:** Maintains optimal level of challenge to keep learners engaged.

---

### Khanmigo (Khan Academy)

**Type:** AI tutor and teaching assistant powered by GPT-4

**Platform:** Integrates with Khan Academy's world-class content library

**Capabilities by User:**

| User | Features |
|------|----------|
| **Students** | Personalized assistance in math, reading, writing, computer science; coaching; test preparation |
| **Teachers** | Create effective lesson plans and tasks based on student assessments |
| **Parents** | Monitor chat activities; receive explanations of subjects their child is studying to assist with schoolwork |

**Availability:** After piloting in selected US schools, now available to the public as an always-available chatbot.

---

### Nuance Dragon Speech Recognition

**Type:** Speech recognition software for education

**Capability:** Transcribes up to 160 words per minute with effortless accuracy

**Benefits:**
- Teachers can cut time creating lesson plans and syllabi
- Students can improve spelling and word recognition
- Especially beneficial for users with accessibility requirements
- Navigate documents by voice

---

### StepWise (Querium)

**Type:** AI-powered STEM education platform

**Mission:** Revolutionizing how students learn STEM subjects

**Features:**
- AI-based analytics to accurately assess student progress
- Personalized instructions based on individual learning needs
- Machine learning algorithms to uncover difficult concepts
- Interactive lessons
- Adaptive quizzes
- Real-time feedback

---

### Carnegie Learning - Cognitive Tutor

**Type:** AI-based educational software for K-12 schools

**Focus:** Curriculum and assessment materials

**Key Technology:** Adapts teaching approach to individual student performance using artificial intelligence

**Benefits:**
- Personalized instructions through adaptive learning techniques
- Dynamic feedback loop for teachers to track student progress
- Informed decisions about lesson plans
